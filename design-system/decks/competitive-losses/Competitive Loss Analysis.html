The response
5

Five plays to lose fewer deals

10
The plays · 1 & 2

Get there first. Then win the economics.

01

Engage earlier, move faster

"Caught too late" is our most common competitive killer. Multi-thread the deal, lock a specific next step every call, and never let "send me info" end a conversation.

02

Reframe the economics

Don't get out-bid on upfront cash. Run the model early; reframe top-line split to take-home after overhead; lead with the three wealth vehicles — book, business, and equity.

Together these address ~42 losses a year (timing + economics). Speed and a confident comp conversation are the highest-ROI behaviors.

11
The plays · 3 & 4

Beat Farther on structure. Convert the fence-sitters.

03

Out-position Farther

Own the system-of-record and AI-native story; surface their right-of-first-refusal and PE pressure. Use the proof point: ~1 in 4 advisors who leave Farther come to Savvy — offer the intro.

04

Convert timing & fear of change

Flip "I'm not unhappy" to "not unhappy isn't thriving." Bring the spouse in early, anchor urgency to a real catalyst, and treat fear of change as a coaching moment, not a dead end.

Farther + fear-of-change together represent ~31 losses. The battle card and discovery playbook already exist — the gap is consistent execution.

12
The plays · 5 & the guardrail

Tighten the handoff — and protect the bar

05

Win the SGA → SGM handoff

Surface who else is in the mix and the move-mindset before the intro call. A miscategorized deal is a lost deal. (See the Competitive Deals Playbook.)

✓

Keep the quality bar high

The 23% we declined — non-transferable books, sub-scale revenue — is the system working. Disqualify fast and spend the time on winnable deals.

Losing less isn't about winning every deal — it's about winning the right ones faster, and walking from the rest without burning cycles.
