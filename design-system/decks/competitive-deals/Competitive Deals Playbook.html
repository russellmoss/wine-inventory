Move 01 · Discovery

Surface the competition

You can't position against something you haven't named. Ask with curiosity, not defensiveness — map the field early so the SGM walks in prepared.

"Got it — so who else are you talking to right now? Just trying to get a sense of what's in the mix."
"Are the other firms you're looking at structured similarly to us, or are you exploring different models?"
"You mentioned a local RIA — I want my colleague to know that going into the next call, because it changes how we'd position things."
04
Move 02 · Move mindset

Active shopper, or passive responder?

Passive
Picked up the phone — hasn't really shopped
  • You have relative access and a strong team. Use it.
  • Hard-close the SGM meeting on this call.
  • Frame the SGM intro around curiosity, not pain.
Active
Talking to multiple firms right now
  • Get who else is in the mix — and their model.
  • Ask if they've floated the move with best clients.
  • Close harder on urgency; flag any local-RIA name.

A miscategorized mindset is one of the most common SGA → SGM friction points. When in doubt, ask: "Have you actually decided to move, or are you still feeling it out?"

05
Move 03 · The handoff

What the SGM must know
before the intro call

Mindset

Active vs. passive — it sets the entire tone of the SGM's intro call (curiosity vs. pain framing).

Who's in the mix

Every competing firm named, and whether their model is similar to Savvy's or fundamentally different.

The local-RIA flag

Local offers things Savvy can't — office space, in-person teams. The SGM needs to weigh how much that matters.

Timing & blockers

Payout schedules, renewals, non-competes — anything that changes urgency or the path to a "yes."

Closest fight · Farther & Compound

"Savvy doesn't feel that different"

Acknowledge the surface similarity, then move to where it matters. Don't manufacture urgency on a "not unhappy" advisor — flip it: "Not unhappy and genuinely excited about where you're headed are two different things."

System of record

Savvy owns it; Farther doesn't. Unified data is what lets AI work across the whole business — theirs is fragmented.

People vs. technology

Farther throws people at problems — their family-office offering is people-based with no data flow between systems.

Whose customer?

09
Battle card · the structural reframes

Farther: look past the nominal number

Right of first refusal

Farther can match the price if an advisor ever sells their book — it locks in their optionality. Savvy has none; sell to anyone.

PE pressure

Their first PE round (General Atlantic) carries revenue-target hooks & 3–5 yr return clocks. Savvy's venture backers take 10–15 yr views.

The tech stack

Farther's platform runs on HubSpot with an overlay — that's what limits margin and the multiples that drive real equity upside.

Equity structure, not size

A bigger nominal grant at a higher strike isn't more value. Compass spreads 4 grants over ~7 yrs; Savvy is one grant, one low strike, 5-yr vest.

Frame: it's not "how much equity" — it's "what is the equity actually worth, and where can it go." Always offer to connect them with an advisor who made the move.

Objection · "My firm already has AI"

A note-taker isn't an operating system

Farther Google Gemini
An investor-driven bet on a general LLM, running on fragmented data. We chose Claude for complex financial reasoning — beholden to no investor's stack.
Jump Meeting assistant
Great at summarizing meetings. Knows what was said — not what to do about it. A point solution, not a platform.
LPL Claude — announced
Announced a partnership; nothing has shipped. A patchwork of third-party tools bolted onto a legacy platform. We're already in production.
Altruist Hazel Custodian add-on
A custodian-locked chatbot that reads what you hand it. It can't act on data it can't see. Savvy Intelligence knows your whole book.

Structural argument: AI can't exist without organized data. Only a unified system of record can deliver AI across the entire advisor experience — bolt-on tools can't, no matter the investment.

11
Wirehouse · IBD · Traditional RIA

The reframes that move legacy advisors

Wirehouse → "You are the brand"

Full independence with enterprise infrastructure. An advisor under 40 in a captive model is in the worst seat for their profession.

IBD → split vs. take-home

"Freedom's great, high split — but you get what you pay for." Reframe top-line split to bottom-line after overhead, tech, staff, marketing.

Social proof that lands

Marc Casady, former CEO of LPL, is one of Savvy's biggest investors — and Savvy is the only fintech in his portfolio.

Timing the disruption

LPL's acquisition of Commonwealth created real culture friction. Commonwealth advisors who valued that community are open right now.

12
When it becomes a bidding war

Reset the max-payout shopper

"If you're just about the dollars, we might not be the right place for you. The advisors who join with us are thinking about the next phase — and when you see all of that and then we put the economics together, that's the sweet spot."

It resets the conversation around partnership value, signals Savvy is selective, and puts the advisor on their heels. Then make the bigger point:

You own your book · your business · and equity in Savvy — three wealth-building vehicles no single split can match.
13
The golden rules

How we win competitive deals

Do

  • Ask directly who else is in the mix — early.
  • Acknowledge good firms; compete on where it matters.
  • Reframe to value: data, AI, independence, equity.
  • Use social proof and offer advisor-to-advisor intros.
  • Flag mindset + competitors in every SGA → SGM handoff.

Don't

  • Pretend the competition isn't there.
  • Bash competitors — let the structure speak.
  • Manufacture urgency on a "not unhappy" advisor.
  • Let it become a pure payout bidding war.
  • Quote specific valuation multiples to advisors.
