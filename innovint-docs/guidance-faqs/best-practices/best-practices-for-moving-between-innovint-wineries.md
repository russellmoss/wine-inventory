# Best Practices for Moving Between InnoVint Wineries

**Moving your wine from one InnoVint facility to another? Here are the Best Practices and Tips for Recording Bond to Bond Transfers between InnoVint Wineries.**

Optional steps are especially relevant if moving wineries.

*Important:* The person recording the B2B transfers must have either Admin or Team Member capabilities at both the originating and destination wineries.

*Special Note:* If you are a client at a custom crush facility, work with your host at the destination winery to ensure that your bond # and owner tag are set up in their account.

### **Steps at the Originating Winery**

**Step 1:** Export all historical data to retain for records

- Find a list of available exports [HERE](https://support.innovint.us/hc/en-us/articles/213128166-what-exports-are-available-in-innovint-vessel_expl?hsLang=en)
- Recommended: All Lot & Vessel exports, the Winery Activity Feed, Bottlings, & Fruit Intake reports, as well as any Custom Analysis Reports

**Step 2:** (Optional) Before recording bond to bond transfers (Step 3), place a tag on all barrels and other vessels that are part of the transfer. Example: “Bulk Archive”. Use the [bulk add tags](https://support.innovint.us/hc/en-us/articles/204503449-adding-editing-or-removing-tags?hsLang=en) function to do this.

**Step 3:** Record Bond to Bond Transfer to another InnoVint Winery

*Note:* This action will both *empty* and *Archive* each of the transferred lots. It will create a NEW lot code at the recipient winery.

See how to record “B2B to another InnoVint winery” [HERE](https://support.innovint.us/hc/en-us/articles/360018542692-bond-to-bond-transfers?hsLang=en#B2B_IV).

- This must be repeated for all lots (one lot per action).
  - The following information transfers with the lot.
    - Lot code/name
    - Lot composite analysis
    - Vineyard/Block composition(must match exactly or a new vineyard source will be created)
    - Notes on the lot
    - If costing is activated, then current cost data per category is transferred (not the full lot cost history)

**Step 4:** (Optional - see Step 2) Archive vessels that are physically removed

- Use the [archive multiple vessels](https://support.innovint.us/hc/en-us/articles/205039485-archive-lots-and-vessels?hsLang=en) function and look up vessels by the “Bulk Archive” tag you added earlier.
- Archive all the barrels and other vessels that moved out with the shipment

### **Steps at the Destination Winery**

**Step 1:** [Input newly received vessels](https://support.innovint.us/hc/en-us/204106559-step-1-enter-your-vessels-into-the-system-with-video-?hsLang=en)

*Tip!* Speed things up by [importing your vessels via .csv](https://support.innovint.us/hc/en-us/how-to-import-vessels-via-csv-file?hsLang=en)

**Step 2:** Receive volume into bond

See how to record the “B2B Transfer In” action [HERE](https://support.innovint.us/hc/en-us/articles/360018542692-bond-to-bond-transfers?hsLang=en).

- Fill the lot and associated vessels with volume
- Add a +Note with the transfer details, such as bond #, address, etc.

**Step 3:** (optional)

- Go to the lot details page and [+Add note](https://support.innovint.us/hc/en-us/articles/360009672091-how-to-add-a-photo-note?hsLang=en)
- Save any screen captures of the lot history from the originating winery and add them as a photo note
- Or, save csv exports from the originating winery as PDFs and add as a PDF note

**Step 4:** (optional)

- Use the per lot analysis exports from the originating winery to record an [Analysis Import](https://support.innovint.us/hc/en-us/articles/115002687291-how-to-import-analyses-via-csv?hsLang=en) for each corresponding lot at the destination winery.
- or Reference import templates [HERE](https://support.innovint.us/hc/en-us/articles/115002684812-analysis-import-format-guidelines-for-csv-file?hsLang=en)

**Step 5**: (optional)

[Input any dry goods and additive batches](https://support.innovint.us/hc/en-us/articles/115000825066-how-to-create-additives-and-additive-batches?hsLang=en) to the Additive Explorer. Or make quick work of it and [import your packaging via .csv](https://support.innovint.us/hc/en-us/how-to-import-dry-goods?hsLang=en)!

**Step 6:** (optional)

Follow the steps [HERE](/hc/en-us/articles/115001540266-ets-integration-overview#how) to link your ETS account at the destination winery (only available if they have the ETS Integration enabled).
