# Fortification and Amelioration

To fortify, or ameliorate your wine lot requires a few simple Volume Adjustments.

- To review how to sweeten wines, please see our article on [Sweetening Workflows.](https://support.innovint.us/hc/en-us/sweetening?hsLang=en)
- To add sugar or concentrate to undeclared juice or must in order to increase potential alcohol, please see our article on [Chaptalization](//innovint-6865708.hs-sites.com/hc/en-us/articles/360021173511-chaptalization?hsLang=en).
- To add water to juice or must (while a juice lot is in tax class *Fermenting Juice*), please see our article on [how to record a water addition](//innovint-6865708.hs-sites.com/hc/en-us/articles/115001418631-how-to-record-a-water-addition?hsLang=en).

**Note:** Wine, and spirits must be in the correct **declared** tax class to populate the TTB report correctly. Lots still under the Fermenting Juice class will only affect Part VII of the report.

This article covers:

- [Fortification](#fortification)
- [Amelioration](#amelioration)

### Fortification

To record the addition of brandy or spirits for fortification:

1. Record a Volume Adjustment to remove the volume from the wine lot.
   ![Fortification and Amelioration-order](https://support.innovint.us/hs-fs/hubfs/Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-order.webp?width=688&height=31&name=Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-order.webp) ![Fortification and Amelioration-record](https://support.innovint.us/hs-fs/hubfs/Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-record.webp?width=688&height=418&name=Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-record.webp)
2. Uncheck the box for archiving. You will need this lot to be active for Step 3.
3. Record a Volume Adjustment to remove the required volume from your 'spirits' lot
   ![Fortification and Amelioration-order2](https://support.innovint.us/hs-fs/hubfs/Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-order2.webp?width=688&height=31&name=Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-order2.webp) ![Fortification and Amelioration-volume adj](https://support.innovint.us/hs-fs/hubfs/Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-volume%20adj.webp?width=688&height=394&name=Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-volume%20adj.webp)
4. Record a Volume Adjustment to add the total volume after fortification
   ![Fortification and Amelioration-order3](https://support.innovint.us/hs-fs/hubfs/Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-order3.webp?width=688&height=31&name=Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-order3.webp) ![Fortification and Amelioration-adj after fort](https://support.innovint.us/hs-fs/hubfs/Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-adj%20after%20fort.webp?width=688&height=418&name=Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-adj%20after%20fort.webp)

Don't forget to verify the alcohol content after the addition of wine spirits and change the tax class accordingly.

Any volume adjustment that completely empties a lot will also completely remove the additives within that lot. This workflow will impact the  [Calculated Additives](https://support.innovint.us/hc/en-us/calculated-additives?hsLang=en) on the juice/wine lot. You will be able to access them via the Removed Additives export for the volume adjustment, and can re-enter them via an Addition action *making sure to update Amount Removed to 0 on the addition*.

### Amelioration

*To add water to juice or must (while a juice lot is in tax class Fermenting Juice), please see our article on [how to record a water addition](//innovint-6865708.hs-sites.com/hc/en-us/articles/115001418631-how-to-record-a-water-addition?hsLang=en).*

To increase the volume of a finished, ***declared*** wine lot due to a water add:

1. Record a Volume Adjustment to remove the entire fill from the wine lot![Fortification and Amelioration-order4](https://support.innovint.us/hs-fs/hubfs/Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-order4.webp?width=688&height=31&name=Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-order4.webp)![Fortification and Amelioration-record amelioration](https://support.innovint.us/hs-fs/hubfs/Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-record%20amelioration.webp?width=688&height=404&name=Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-record%20amelioration.webp)
2. (*Optional)* Record a Custom Action (or assign a Custom Task in a work order)![Fortification and Amelioration-order5](https://support.innovint.us/hs-fs/hubfs/Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-order5.webp?width=655&height=30&name=Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-order5.webp)![Fortification and Amelioration-custom water add](https://support.innovint.us/hs-fs/hubfs/Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-custom%20water%20add.webp?width=688&height=367&name=Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-custom%20water%20add.webp)
3. Record a Volume Adjustment to add the total gallons of the lot after the water addition![Fortification and Amelioration-order6](https://support.innovint.us/hs-fs/hubfs/Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-order6.webp?width=688&height=31&name=Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-order6.webp)![Fortification and Amelioration-vol adj after water add](https://support.innovint.us/hs-fs/hubfs/Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-vol%20adj%20after%20water%20add.webp?width=688&height=400&name=Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-vol%20adj%20after%20water%20add.webp)

Any volume adjustment that completely empties a lot will also completely remove the additives within that lot. This workflow will impact the  [Calculated Additives](https://support.innovint.us/hc/en-us/calculated-additives?hsLang=en) on the juice/wine lot. You will be able to access them via the Removed Additives export for the volume adjustment, and can re-enter them via an Addition action *making sure to update Amount Removed to 0 on the addition*.

\*\* Following these steps will report on Page 1, Columns (a), (b), and (c), lines 6 and 21,  of the 5120.17 TTB Report.![Fortification and Amelioration-ttb](https://support.innovint.us/hs-fs/hubfs/Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-ttb.webp?width=688&height=470&name=Fortification%20and%20Amelioration-ttb.webp)
