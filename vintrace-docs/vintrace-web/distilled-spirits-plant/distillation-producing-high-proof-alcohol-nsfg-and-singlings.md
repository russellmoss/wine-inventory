# Distillation: Producing High Proof Alcohol (NSFG) and Singlings

This functionality is available starting with [vintrace 9.4.3](https://support.vintrace.com/hc/en-us/articles/32303276816020), but not enabled by default. If you would like to use this functionality, please contact our support team.

Be sure to complete the [configuration steps for a Distilled Spirits Plant (DSP)](https://support.vintrace.com/hc/en-us/articles/32301312232852) before starting this workflow.

![Diagram - Distillation Producing NSFG and Singlings 20231108.png](https://support.vintrace.com/hc/article_attachments/32329220938900)

After the distilling material has been moved to the DSP Winery and you’re ready to produce the high proof alcohol (NSFG) and singlings, record a one-to-many transfer.

When recording the transfer:

- Select a source vessel in the Production account for the distilling material (*Column dm*) tax class.
- Be sure to select empty destination vessel(s) in the storage - domestic account.
- At least one of the destination vessels will be for the high proof alcohol in the *Column i or j* tax class.
- The other destination vessel will be for the singlings (heads cut and fusil oil cut) in the *Column j* tax class.

Both the high proof alcohol and singlings produced will be moved to the storage - domestic account. The DSP tax events will be included in the [TTB Monthly Report of Production Operations](https://www.ttb.gov/images/pdfs/forms/f511040.pdf) and the [Monthly Report of Storage Operations](https://www.ttb.gov/images/pdfs/forms/f511011.pdf).
