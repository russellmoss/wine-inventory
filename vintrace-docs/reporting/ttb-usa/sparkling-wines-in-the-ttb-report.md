# Sparkling Wines in the TTB Report

Because sparkling wine is defined as a special [tax class](https://support.vintrace.com/hc/en-us/articles/32301306220180), it’s reported differently on the [TTB Report](https://support.vintrace.com/hc/en-us/articles/32303292459668) (5120.17).

To illustrate where different sparkling operations show on the TTB Report, we created a scenario where already fermented wine was brought in in bulk, tiraged, and disgorged within a single month.

Although our scenario is unlikely in the real world, we created it to show all three operations on the 5120.17 for a single month.

Suppose we received 10,000 gallons of sparkling wine in bulk . When we recorded the [bulk intake](https://support.vintrace.com/hc/en-us/articles/32303303281428), the tax class is under 14% and the tax class is set to *Sparkling Wine*.

![mceclip0.png](https://support.vintrace.com/hc/article_attachments/32328979452692)

![mceclip1.png](https://support.vintrace.com/hc/article_attachments/32328979474964)

Next, we perform a tirage operation for 350 bottles produced by fermentation and bottled with a small loss.

![mceclip3.png](https://support.vintrace.com/hc/article_attachments/32328985556116)

We then disgorge against the tirage group to move the sparkling wine from bulk to bottled in vintrace.

![mceclip4.png](https://support.vintrace.com/hc/article_attachments/32328979529108)

Here’s how the three operations show in the TTB Report:

- Line 7, column e shows our initial intake of 10000 gallons.
- Line 13, column e shows our disgorged amount.
- Line 20, column e shows our small loss on tirage.
